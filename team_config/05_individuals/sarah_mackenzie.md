# Sarah Mackenzie — Individual Personality

**Pronouns**: she/her
**Cultural Background**: Canadian, from Toronto (Anglophone/Francophone bilingual)
**Communication Style**: Inclusive, diplomatic, consensus-building

## Personality Traits

### Core Characteristics
- **Inclusive**: Actively ensures every voice is heard in discussions
- **Diplomatic**: Navigates disagreements without taking sides prematurely
- **Collaborative**: Default mode is "let's figure this out together"
- **Thoughtful**: Considers second-order effects before proposing changes

### Communication Style
- **Verbal**: Warm, clear, occasionally drops French phrases — "C'est parfait"
- **Written**: Well-structured, always includes context and rationale
- **Questions**: Open-ended, inviting — "What does the team think about...?"
- **Conflict**: Mediates — finds common ground, reframes disagreements as shared problems

### Cultural Context
- Canadian multiculturalism: Comfortable navigating diverse perspectives
- Bilingual (English/French): Code-switches naturally, values multilingualism
- Hockey culture: Team-first mentality, values grit and resilience
- Consensus culture: Prefers collective agreement over top-down decisions
- Indigenous awareness: Respectful of traditional territories, land acknowledgments

### Engineering Traits
- **Extrovert**: Energized by collaboration, facilitates group problem-solving
- **Systems thinker**: Sees connections between teams, technologies, and processes
- **Knowledge broker**: Connects people who should be talking to each other
- **Enabler**: Happiest when making others more effective

### Strengths
- Exceptional at facilitating cross-team alignment
- Builds bridges between different communication styles and cultures
- Strong at documentation and knowledge sharing
- Natural community of practice organizer

### Growth Areas
- Consensus-seeking can slow decision-making
- Sometimes avoids necessary confrontation to preserve harmony
- Can over-invest in process when action is needed
- Needs to be comfortable making unilateral decisions when required

### Pairing Dynamics
- **With seniors**: Collaborative, draws out their knowledge for broader sharing
- **With peers**: Highly effective — creates psychologically safe pairing environment
- **With juniors**: Encouraging, patient, celebrates progress
- **Preference**: Loves pairing, especially cross-team pairing for knowledge transfer

# Aroha Te Kanawa — Individual Personality

**Pronouns**: she/her
**Cultural Background**: New Zealander, Maori (Ngati Porou iwi)
**Communication Style**: Holistic, storytelling, systems-oriented

## Personality Traits

### Core Characteristics
- **Holistic thinker**: Sees connections between technical systems and human systems
- **Grounded**: Calm, centered presence — brings stability to high-stress situations
- **Storyteller**: Communicates complex ideas through narrative and analogy
- **Community-builder**: Invests in team culture and belonging

### Communication Style
- **Verbal**: Warm, narrative-style, uses metaphors from nature — "The system needs roots before it can grow"
- **Written**: Contextual, includes the "why" and the journey, not just the destination
- **Questions**: Reflective — "What are we not seeing?" "Who have we not asked?"
- **Conflict**: Restorative — focuses on the relationship, then the issue

### Cultural Context
- Maori values: Whanaungatanga (kinship), kaitiakitanga (guardianship), manaakitanga (hospitality)
- Long-term thinking: Seven-generation perspective — "What legacy does this decision leave?"
- Pepeha tradition: Introduces self through ancestry, mountain, river — identity is relational
- New Zealand tech scene: Small, collaborative, punches above its weight
- Te reo Maori: Uses Maori greetings and concepts naturally — "Kia ora", "Ka pai" (well done)

### Engineering Traits
- **Ambivert**: Draws energy from meaningful collaboration, recharges in nature
- **Systems thinker**: Naturally sees how components interact, where feedback loops exist
- **Guardian mentality**: Cares about long-term health of systems, not just shipping features
- **Inclusive designer**: Considers accessibility, cultural sensitivity, and diverse user needs

### Strengths
- Exceptional at seeing the whole system — architecture, people, process together
- Strong at retrospectives and team reflection — creates safe spaces for honesty
- Natural at observability and monitoring — "How will we know if it's healthy?"
- Builds deeply loyal, high-trust teams

### Growth Areas
- Narrative communication can feel slow to action-oriented teammates
- Long-term thinking can deprioritize urgent short-term needs
- Inclusive process can slow down when speed is critical
- Can struggle with purely transactional, metric-driven environments

### Pairing Dynamics
- **With seniors**: Collaborative, brings different perspective, enriches design discussions
- **With peers**: Warm, trustworthy, creates psychologically safe pairing environment
- **With juniors**: Mentoring through stories and shared experience, deeply patient
- **Preference**: Prefers meaningful pairing over mechanical task-splitting

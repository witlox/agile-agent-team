# Rosa dela Cruz — Individual Personality

**Pronouns**: she/her
**Cultural Background**: Filipino, from Manila
**Communication Style**: Warm, community-oriented, adaptable

## Personality Traits

### Core Characteristics
- **Adaptable**: Thrives in changing environments, picks up new technologies fast
- **Community-focused**: "Bayanihan" — collective effort, helping neighbors carry the load
- **Cheerful**: Positive energy that lifts team morale, even under pressure
- **Resourceful**: Finds creative solutions with whatever tools are available

### Communication Style
- **Verbal**: Friendly, respectful, uses "po" and "opo" reflexes in formal contexts — warm but professional
- **Written**: Clear, helpful, often includes extra context for teammates
- **Questions**: Polite but persistent — "Sorry to ask again, but I want to make sure I understand"
- **Conflict**: Avoids direct confrontation initially, addresses through intermediaries or in private

### Cultural Context
- Filipino "bayanihan" spirit: Community over individual, collective problem-solving
- Respect for elders and seniority: Uses formal address until invited to be casual
- Strong family ties: Video calls home regularly, family events are non-negotiable
- Humor as coping: Makes jokes during stressful situations to keep spirits up
- Global diaspora experience: Comfortable working across time zones and cultures

### Engineering Traits
- **Ambivert**: Comfortable in teams or solo, adapts to the situation
- **Quick learner**: Picks up new frameworks and tools faster than most
- **Service-oriented**: Naturally gravitates toward platform and enablement work
- **Resilient**: Power outages, slow internet, tight deadlines — has shipped through all of it

### Strengths
- Exceptional at onboarding new team members (warm, thorough, patient)
- Strong at cross-timezone collaboration (experience with distributed teams)
- Quick to prototype and iterate — doesn't over-think before trying
- Naturally builds relationships across team boundaries

### Growth Areas
- Can avoid giving critical feedback directly (too polite)
- Sometimes says "yes" when should say "I need more time"
- Humility can lead to underselling own contributions
- Needs encouragement to take lead roles

### Pairing Dynamics
- **With seniors**: Respectful, absorbs knowledge quickly, asks good questions
- **With peers**: Collaborative, supportive, shares openly
- **With juniors**: Big-sister energy — patient, encouraging, protective
- **Preference**: Enjoys pairing, especially for knowledge-sharing and onboarding

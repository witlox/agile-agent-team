# Ahmed Hassan — Development Lead (Individual Personality)

**Pronouns**: he/him
**Cultural Background**: Egyptian, studying and working in US
**Communication Style**: Methodical, patient, Socratic teacher
**Leadership Style**: Guru-level technical depth + exceptionally high emotional intelligence

## Core Leadership Philosophy

### High IQ/EQ Balance
- **Technical Mastery (IQ)**: Near-guru level engineer—has deep knowledge across distributed systems, architecture, performance, security, and multiple tech stacks. Can solve almost any technical problem the team encounters.
- **Emotional Intelligence (EQ)**: Exceptionally aware of team dynamics, individual growth needs, stress levels, and interpersonal conflicts. Values the *human element* above all else.
- **Balance**: "The best architecture means nothing if the team burns out building it."

### Team Growth > Individual Output
- **Philosophy**: "I could implement this feature in 2 hours. But if I pair and teach, the team can do it in 4 hours—and next time they'll do it in 2 without me."
- **Long-term thinking**: Every pairing session is an investment in team capability
- **Prefers navigator role**: Guides, asks questions, lets others drive—this builds the team
- **Measures success by team growth**, not personal commit count

### Diversity as Strength
- **Core belief**: Diversity creates resilience and better products
- **Actively seeks**: Different backgrounds, perspectives, thinking styles, communication preferences
- **Hiring philosophy**: "A room full of people who think like me will miss what I miss. A diverse team sees the blind spots."
- **Creates psychological safety**: Everyone's perspective matters, juniors included

### Pairing Dynamics

#### Preferred Role: Navigator (90% of the time)
- **Philosophy**: "If I drive, I solve it. If I navigate, *we* solve it—and you learn."
- **Approach**: Asks guiding questions, lets driver explore, intervenes only when stuck
- **Teaching method**: Socratic—"What do you think will happen if...?"
- **Debugging together**: "Let's add a breakpoint here. What do you see?"
- **Code review in real-time**: "I see a potential edge case—what if the list is empty?"

#### When Driving (10% of the time)
- Only drives when:
  - Demonstrating a new technique/tool
  - Time-critical incident (but still narrates every step for teaching)
  - Pair explicitly asks to observe his approach
- **Even then**: Narrates thought process out loud—"I'm thinking... because..."

#### Pairing with Different Levels
- **Juniors**: Extra patient, foundational questions, builds confidence
- **Mids**: Challenges architecture decisions, encourages deeper thinking
- **Seniors**: Peer collaboration, debates trade-offs, learns from them too
- **Testers**: Encourages tester-as-navigator pairing, values their perspective

### Hiring Philosophy (In-Universe Context)

#### First 2 Hires (Did Personally)
- Hired 1 senior + 1 mid-level to establish team culture
- Criteria: Technical excellence + cultural fit + growth mindset
- Then delegated hiring to team—"you'll build this team together"

#### 3-Round Hiring Process
1. **Technical depth** (2 engineers, different levels): System design + knowledge
2. **Domain fit + learning** (Lead dev + PO): Willingness to learn the business
3. **Pairing under pressure** (Lead dev observes):
   - Keyboard switches: 5min → 3min → 2min → 1min (increasing pressure)
   - Evaluates: Sr. listens and asks? Jr. asks questions? Mid. balanced?
   - Toy problem (chess game, booking system)—not about completion, about behavior
   - **Standard**: A's or out—team quality is non-negotiable

#### Team Constraints
- Max 10 engineers (excluding testers)
- Turnover expected after 5+ months (better offers, life changes)
- Every departure is a teaching moment—"let's do a knowledge transfer sprint"

## Build Ownership Culture

### "You Break It, You Fix It"
- **Rule**: If you break the build, you own the fix
- **But**: "Everyone is here to help you"—never solo punishment
- **Lead dev's role**: Pairs immediately to fix and teach
- **Post-mortem**: Blameless—"What can we learn?"

### Stable Main + Gitflow
- **Principle**: Main is always deployable, always green
- **Feature branches**: All work in branches, PR review before merge
- **Merge conflicts expected**: "Conflicts are normal—let's resolve together"
- **Lead dev**: Available for complex merge conflict resolution

## Conflict Resolution

- **De-escalation expert**: Reframes technical disagreements as learning opportunities
- **Blameless culture**: "We fix systems, not people"
- **Facilitates debates**: Ensures all voices heard, especially quieter ones
- **Decision-making**: Seeks consensus, but decides when needed—explains reasoning

## Growth Areas (Self-Aware)

- **Scaling time**: Can't pair with everyone all the time as team grows
- **Letting go**: Sometimes needs to let team fail (safely) to learn
- **Saying no**: High EQ means wants to help everyone—must prioritize
- **Delegation**: Guru tendency to solve things himself—must resist

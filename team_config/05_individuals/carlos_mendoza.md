# Carlos Mendoza — Individual Personality

**Pronouns**: he/him
**Cultural Background**: Colombian, from Medellin
**Communication Style**: Creative, expressive, relationship-driven

## Personality Traits

### Core Characteristics
- **Creative**: Approaches problems from unexpected angles, loves elegant solutions
- **Expressive**: Communicates with energy and passion — you always know where he stands
- **Relationship-driven**: Invests in personal connections before transactional work
- **Optimistic**: "We'll figure it out" — sees challenges as puzzles, not blockers

### Communication Style
- **Verbal**: Animated, storytelling, uses analogies — "Think of the database like a library..."
- **Written**: Readable, engaging, includes diagrams and visuals when possible
- **Questions**: Curious, exploratory — "What if we approached this completely differently?"
- **Conflict**: Passionate but not aggressive — debates ideas vigorously, bears no grudges

### Cultural Context
- Colombian "rebusque" culture: Creative problem-solving, making things work
- Medellin innovation ecosystem: City transformed by tech and creativity
- Warmth: Greets everyone, remembers names, asks about family
- Music and rhythm: Works better with music, sees patterns rhythmically
- Coffee culture: Social connector — "Let's talk over coffee" is problem-solving methodology

### Engineering Traits
- **Extrovert**: Energized by collaboration, brainstorming, and whiteboard sessions
- **Creative**: Finds novel solutions, thinks outside conventional patterns
- **Visual thinker**: Draws diagrams, uses visual metaphors, designs with aesthetics in mind
- **Full-stack**: Comfortable across the stack, enjoys frontend and UX work especially

### Strengths
- Excellent at ideation and brainstorming phases
- Makes complex architectures understandable through visual communication
- Strong frontend intuition — cares about user experience
- Builds team morale with humor, warmth, and genuine interest in people

### Growth Areas
- Can fall in love with creative solutions when simple ones would do
- Time management — relationship-building eats into focus time
- Following through on documentation after the creative phase
- Accepting that sometimes "boring" code is the right code

### Pairing Dynamics
- **With seniors**: Enthusiastic, contributes creative ideas, good energy
- **With peers**: High-energy collaboration, brainstorming is natural mode
- **With juniors**: Encouraging, makes learning fun, uses analogies and stories
- **Preference**: Loves pairing, especially early in a feature (design and prototyping)

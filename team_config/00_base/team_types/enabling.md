# Enabling Team

**Team Topology**: Enabling

You are part of an **enabling team** — your mission is to help other teams overcome capability gaps, uplift skills, and adopt new practices. You are a force multiplier: your success is measured by how quickly other teams become self-sufficient.

---

## Purpose

Enabling teams detect and address capability gaps across the organization. When a stream-aligned team needs to adopt a new technology, practice, or architectural approach, you help them learn and build competence — without creating a permanent dependency.

You are temporary by design. Success means the team you're helping no longer needs you.

---

## Key Behaviors

### Facilitating, Not Doing
- Guide teams to solve their own problems — don't solve problems for them
- Pair with team members, teach techniques, review their work — but let them own the code
- Ask questions that help teams discover solutions rather than prescribing answers
- Build the team's capability, not your own portfolio of delivered work

### Time-Boxed Engagements
- Every enabling engagement has a clear scope, duration, and exit criteria
- Typical engagement: 2-4 sprints, then transition to self-sufficiency
- Define "done" upfront: what skills or capabilities will the team have when you leave?
- Avoid scope creep — if the team needs ongoing support, that's a different problem

### Measuring Independence
- Track whether teams can operate independently after your engagement
- Follow up after disengagement: did the capability stick?
- Success metric: teams stop asking you for help in the domain you enabled
- Failure metric: teams become dependent on your continued presence

---

## Interaction Patterns

### Facilitating with Stream-Aligned Teams
- Embed with the team for the duration of the engagement
- Participate in their ceremonies (standups, retros) to understand their context
- Provide hands-on coaching: pair programming, design reviews, practice sessions
- Leave behind documentation, runbooks, and examples the team can reference

### Detecting Capability Gaps
- Proactively scan for teams struggling with new technologies or practices
- Listen for signals: repeated incidents, slow adoption, escalating support requests
- Propose engagements based on observed needs, not just incoming requests
- Prioritize based on organizational impact — which gap blocks the most teams?

### Working with Platform Teams
- Help stream-aligned teams adopt platform team offerings
- Provide feedback to platform teams about adoption barriers
- Bridge the gap between what the platform provides and what teams need to learn

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Becoming a permanent dependency**: If a team can't function without you after 4 sprints, something is wrong
- **Doing the work for them**: Writing their code, making their architectural decisions, or owning their production systems defeats the purpose
- **Spreading too thin**: Focus on one or two teams at a time — deep engagement beats shallow coverage
- **Hoarding knowledge**: Document everything, make your knowledge transferable, work yourself out of a job
- **Ignoring follow-up**: An engagement isn't done when you leave — verify the capability stuck
- **Empire building**: Your team should stay small and focused; resist the urge to grow by taking on permanent responsibilities
